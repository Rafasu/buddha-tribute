Siddharta Gautama

The man who founded Buddhism.

Siddharta's Life

  • 490 || 448 BC - Siddharta Gautama is born.
  • 461 BCE - Around age 29, Gautama had a personal crisis. Realizes he is not inmune from suffering of old age, disease and death.
  • 461 || 460 BC - Leaves home in search for a solution.
  • 461-455 BC - Practices Yoga and experiments extreme asceticism for about 6 years, in search for answers.
  • 455 BC - After realizing ascetism will not help him resolve the problem of human suffering, Gautama sits under a ficus tree and starts meditating.
  • 455 BC - Becomes Buddha.
  • 455 BC - Gives his first sermon in a deer park called Sarnath, near Varanasi.
  • 455 BC - Explain the four noble truths, the eightfold path towars ending suffering, and the middle way.
  • 455-454 BC - Gains his first followers, Sariputra and Mahamaudgalyayana, and the monastic community of Sangha is established.
  • 455-410 BC - Preaches about 45 years around northern India.
  • 410 BC - Gautama dies, asks his disciples to continue spreading his teachings.

After Siddharta

  • 386 BC - Buddhism splinters into two schools, the traditional Sthaviravada line and the more controversial Mahasanghika school, a . school that treats Gautama as a God.
  • 261- BC - Emperor Ashoka's adopt's Buddhism as India's state religion. Buddhism spreads in Asia.
  • 100 AC - The Mahayana school emerges, a more flexible buddhism. Mahayana Buddhism is found in Nepal, Tibet, China, Japan, Mongolia, Korea, and Vietnam.
  • 520 AC - First Zen patriarch Bodhidharma arrives in China.
  • 617 AC - Buddhism is introduced to the Tibet, both Chinese and Indian Buddhists attempt to influence its development. Later Tibet chooses to follow Indian buddhism(Vajrayana).
  • 794 AC - Buddhism is introduced to the west.
  • 1000 AC - Theravada buddhism established in Southeast Asia.
  • 1959 AC - The Tibetan uprising increases western interest in Tibetan Buddhism.
